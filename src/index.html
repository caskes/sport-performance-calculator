Physiology

24%

How efficiently your body converts food energy into mechanical work. 24 % is the standard default for most trained cyclists and runners. Recreational athletes are typically 20–22 %; elite athletes can reach 26–28 %. A higher value lowers the kcal burn estimate.

60 g/hr

Carbohydrates you plan to consume per hour of effort. 30–60 g/hr suits most training rides and runs under 2 h. For long-distance events (> 2.5 h) aim for 60–90 g/hr. Elite endurance athletes with trained guts can absorb up to 120 g/hr using mixed carb sources.

Altitude Hypoxia Modeling

Applies a ~1 % aerobic power loss per 100 m above 1500 m, plus a 5 % metabolic cost penalty above 1800 m (respiratory stress). Critical for Alpine events like Livigno or high-altitude marathon courses. Requires VO₂ Max to be set.
